1 S.36
2 Introduced by Senators Lyons and Gulick
3 Referred to Committee on
4 Date:
5 Subject: Human services; Medicaid, substance use disorder; mental health;
6 long-term residential treatment
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to require the
8 Agency of Human Services to provide coverage for long-term residential
9 treatment to Medicaid beneficiaries with substance use disorder and a co-
10 occurring mental health condition.
11 An act relating to Medicaid coverage of long-term residential treatment for
12 co-occurring substance use disorder and mental health condition
13 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
14 Sec. 1. 33 V.S.A. § 1901m is added to read:
15 § 1901m. LONG-TERM RESIDENTIAL TREATMENT FOR SUBSTANCE
16 USE DISORDER AND CO-OCCURRING MENTAL HEALTH
17 CONDITION
18 (a) The Agency of Human Services shall provide coverage for medically
19 necessary long-term residential treatment to Medicaid beneficiaries with
20 substance use disorder and a co-occurring mental health condition when long-
VT LEG #378522 v.1
BILL AS INTRODUCED S.36
2025 Page 2 of 2
1 term residential treatment is prescribed by a health care professional practicing
2 within the scope of the professional’s license and the residential treatment
3 program is participating in Vermont’s Medicaid program.
4 (b) Coverage provided under this section shall not be for less than:
5 (1) 30 days if the beneficiary’s substance use disorder can be
6 successfully addressed by medication for opioid use disorder; and
7 (2) 60 days if the beneficiary’s substance use disorder cannot be
8 successfully addressed by medication for opioid use disorder.
9 Sec. 2. 33 V.S.A. § 1901m is redesignated to read:
10 § 1901m 1901n. REIMBURSEMENT FOR EMERGENCY MEDICAL
11 SERVICES
12 Sec. 3. EFFECTIVE DATE
13 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
