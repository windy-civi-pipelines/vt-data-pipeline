5 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
6 Sec. 1. 33 V.S.A. § 1901n is added to read:
7 § 1901n. HIGH-INTENSITY RESIDENTIAL TREATMENT FOR
8 SUBSTANCE USE DISORDER AND CO-OCCURRING
9 MENTAL CONDITIONS
10 (a) The Agency of Human Services shall provide coverage for medically
11 necessary high-intensity, medically monitored residential treatment episodes to
12 Medicaid beneficiaries with substance use disorder and a co-occurring mental
13 health condition when medically necessary high-intensity, medically
14 monitored residential treatment episodes are prescribed by a health care
15 professional employed by a residential treatment program who is practicing
16 within the scope of the health professional’s license and the residential
17 treatment program is participating in Vermont’s Medicaid program.
18 (b) Coverage provided under this section shall be for the entire length of
19 stay prescribed by a health care professional employed by a residential
20 treatment program, who shall take into account current best practices for each
21 level of care within the substance use continuum of care.

1 Sec. 6. REPORTS; SERVICES AND PROGRAMMING FOR PERSONS
2 WHO ARE INCAPACITATED
3 (a)(1) The Departments of Health and of Mental Health’s existing plan to
4 expand services and programming for persons who are incapacitated pursuant
5 to 18 V.S.A. § 4810 shall prioritize Chittenden County.
6 (2) On or before February 15, 2026, the Departments of Health and of
7 Mental Health shall jointly provide a presentation to the House Committees on
8 Health Care and on Human Services and to the Senate Committee on Health
9 and Welfare describing efforts to expand services and programming for
10 persons who are incapacitated pursuant to subdivision (1) of this subsection.
11 (b) On or before February 15, 2026, the Department of Corrections shall
12 provide a presentation to the House Committees on Corrections and
13 Institutions, on Health Care, and on Human Services and to the Senate
14 Committees on Institutions and on Health and Welfare describing efforts to
15 reinstate the practice of connecting persons who are in a correctional facility
16 due to incapacitation pursuant to 18 V.S.A. § 4810 with appropriate
17 community-based substance use recovery providers.
18 Sec. 7. REPORTS; HUMAN SERVICES BOARD PROCEEDINGS
19 (a) On or before December 15, 2025, the Agency of Human Services, in
20 consultation with the Human Services Board, Office of the Attorney General,
21 each of the Agency’s departments with cases before the Human Services
