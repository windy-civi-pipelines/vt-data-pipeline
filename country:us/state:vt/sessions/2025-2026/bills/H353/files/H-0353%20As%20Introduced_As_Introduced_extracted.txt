1 H.353
2 Introduced by Representative Logan of Burlington
3 Referred to Committee on
4 Date:
5 Subject: Public service; Public Utility Commission; State energy policy;
6 greenhouse gas emissions; equity
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to incorporate
8 economic equity and beneficial electrification into the State energy policy.
9 An act relating to updates to the State energy policy
10 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
11 Sec. 1. 30 V.S.A. § 202a is amended to read:
12 § 202a. STATE ENERGY POLICY
13 It is the general policy of the State of Vermont:
14 (1) To ensure to the greatest extent practicable that Vermont can meet
15 its energy service needs in a manner that is adequate, reliable, secure, and
16 sustainable; that ensures affordability and equitable distribution of energy
17 burden at the household level, and encourages the State’s economic vitality,
18 the efficient use of energy resources, and cost-effective demand-side
19 management; and that is environmentally sound.
VT LEG #381053 v.1
BILL AS INTRODUCED H.353
2025 Page 2 of 2
1 (2) To identify and evaluate, on an ongoing basis, resources that will
2 meet Vermont’s energy service needs in accordance with the principles of
3 reducing greenhouse gas emissions and least-cost integrated planning,
4 including efficiency, conservation, beneficial electrification, and load
5 management alternatives; wise use of renewable resources; and
6 environmentally sound energy supply.
7 (3) To meet Vermont’s energy service needs in a manner that will
8 achieve the greenhouse gas emissions reductions requirements pursuant to
9 10 V.S.A § 578 and is consistent with the Vermont Climate Action Plan
10 adopted and updated pursuant to 10 V.S.A. § 592.
11 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
12 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
