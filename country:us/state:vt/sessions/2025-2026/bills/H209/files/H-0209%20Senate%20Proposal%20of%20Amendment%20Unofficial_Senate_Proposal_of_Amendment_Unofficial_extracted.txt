5 Sec. 1. 16 V.S.A. § 1388 is amended to read:
6 § 1388. STOCK SUPPLY AND EMERGENCY ADMINISTRATION OF
7 EPINEPHRINE AUTO-INJECTORS
8 (a) As used in this section:
9 (1) “Designated personnel” means a school employee, agent, or
10 volunteer who has completed training required by State Board policy and who
11 has been authorized by the school administrator or delegated by the school
12 nurse to provide and administer epinephrine auto-injectors under in accordance
13 with a provider’s standing order or protocol pursuant to this section and who
14 has completed the training required by State Board policy.
15 (2) “Epinephrine auto-injector” means a U.S. Food and Drug
16 Administration-approved single-use device that delivers a epinephrine delivery
17 system containing a premeasured single dose of epinephrine.
18 (3) “Health care professional” means a physician licensed pursuant to 26
19 V.S.A. chapter 23 or 33, an advanced practice registered nurse licensed to
20 prescribe drugs and medical devices pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 28, or a

1 physician assistant licensed to prescribe drugs and medical devices pursuant to
2 26 V.S.A. chapter 31.
3 (4) “School” means a public or approved independent school and
4 extends to school grounds, school-sponsored activities, school-provided
5 transportation, and school-related programs.
6 (5) “School administrator” means a school’s principal or headmaster.
7 (6) “School nurse” means a school nurse or associate school nurse
8 endorsed by the Agency of Education pursuant to the Licensing of Educators
9 and the Preparation of Educational Professionals rule (CVR 22-000-010).
10 (b)(1) A health care professional may prescribe an epinephrine auto-
11 injector in a school’s name, which may be maintained by the school for use as
12 described in subsection (d) of this section. The health care professional shall
13 issue to the school a standing order for the use of an epinephrine auto-injector
14 prescribed under this section, including protocols for:
15 (A) assessing recognizing whether an individual is experiencing a
16 potentially life-threatening allergic reaction;
17 (B) administering an epinephrine auto-injector to an individual
18 experiencing a potentially life-threatening allergic reaction;
19 (C) caring for an individual after administering an epinephrine auto-
20 injector to him or her, including contacting emergency services personnel and
21 documenting the incident; and

1 (D) disposing of used or expired epinephrine auto-injectors.
2 (2) A pharmacist licensed pursuant to 26 V.S.A. chapter 36 or a health
3 care professional may dispense epinephrine auto-injectors prescribed to a
4 school.
5 (c) A school may maintain a stock supply of epinephrine auto-injectors. A
6 school may enter into arrangements with epinephrine auto-injector
7 manufacturers or suppliers to acquire epinephrine auto-injectors these products
8 for free or at reduced or fair market prices.
9 (d) The school administrator may authorize a school nurse or appropriately
10 trained designated personnel, or both, to:
11 (1) provide an epinephrine auto-injector to a student for self-
12 administration according to a plan of action for managing the student’s life-
13 threatening allergy maintained in the student’s school health records pursuant
14 to section 1387 of this title;
15 (2) administer a prescribed epinephrine auto-injector to a student
16 according to a plan of action maintained in the student’s school health records;
17 and
18 (3) administer an epinephrine auto-injector, in accordance with the
19 protocol issued under subsection (b) of this section, to a student or other
20 individual at a school if the school nurse or designated personnel believe in
21 good faith that the student or individual is experiencing anaphylaxis, regardless

1 of whether the student or individual has a prescription for an epinephrine auto-
2 injector.
3 (e) Designated personnel, a school, a school nurse, and a health care
4 professional prescribing an epinephrine auto-injector to a school shall be
5 immune from any civil or criminal liability arising from the administration or
6 self-administration of an epinephrine auto-injector under this section, unless
7 the person’s conduct constituted intentional misconduct. Providing or
8 administering an epinephrine auto-injector under this section does not
9 constitute the practice of medicine.
10 (f) On or before January 1, 2014, the The State Board, in consultation with
11 the Department of Health, shall adopt policies for managing students with life-
12 threatening allergies and other individuals with life-threatening allergies who
13 may be present at a school. The policies shall:
14 (1) establish protocols to prevent exposure to allergens in schools;
15 (2) establish procedures for responding to life-threatening allergic
16 reactions in schools, including postemergency procedures;
17 (3) implement a process for schools and the parents or guardians of
18 students with a life-threatening allergy to jointly develop a written
19 individualized allergy management plan of action that:
VT LEG #383709 v.1
SENATE PROPOSAL OF AMENDMENT H.209
2025 Page 5 of 5
1 (A) incorporates instructions from a student’s physician health care
2 professional regarding the student’s life-threatening allergy and prescribed
3 treatment;
4 (B) includes the requirements of section 1387 of this title, if a student
5 is authorized to possess and self-administer emergency medication at school;
6 (C) becomes part of the student’s health records maintained by the
7 school; and
8 (D) is updated each school year;
9 (4) require education and training for school nurses and designated
10 personnel, including training related to storing and administering an
11 epinephrine auto-injector and recognizing and responding to a life-threatening
12 allergic reaction; and
13 (5) require each school to make publicly available protocols and
14 procedures developed in accordance with the policies adopted by the State
15 Board under this section.
16 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
17 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
