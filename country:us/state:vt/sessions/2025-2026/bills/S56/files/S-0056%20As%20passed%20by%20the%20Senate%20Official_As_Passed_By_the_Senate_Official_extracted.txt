10 An act relating to creating an Office of New Americans
11 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
12 Sec. 1. OFFICE OF NEW AMERICANS STUDY COMMITTEE; REPORT
13 (a) Creation. There is created the Office of New Americans Study
14 Committee to make recommendations for creating an independent Office of
15 New Americans.
16 (b) Membership. The Committee shall be composed of the following
17 members:
18 (1) the Director of the Vermont Refugee Office, who shall be Chair;

4 Committee on Committees;
5 (4) one member, appointed by the Secretary of Human Services;
6 (5) one member, appointed by the Commissioner of Labor;
7 (6) one member, appointed by the Executive Director of the Office of
8 Racial Equity;
9 (7) one member representing the Association of Africans Living in
10 Vermont; and
11 (8) seven members, appointed by the Governor, who shall include:
12 (A) one member, nominated by the Association of Africans Living in
13 Vermont;
14 (B) one member, nominated by the U.S. Committee for Refugees and
15 Immigrants;
16 (C) one member, nominated by the Brattleboro Development Credit
17 Corporation;
18 (D) one member, nominated by the Vermont Afghan Alliance;
19 (E) one member, nominated by World Education Services;
20 (F) one member, nominated by the Vermont Asylum Assistance
21 Project; and

1 (G) one member with experience or expertise serving nonnative
2 Vermonters on a statewide level.
3 (c) Powers and duties. The Committee shall study and submit a written
4 report to the House Committee on Government Operations and Military
5 Affairs and the Senate Committee on Government Operations concerning
6 recommendations for creating an independent Office of New Americans,
7 including:
8 (1) a summary of the current demographic, economic, and public health
9 data regarding New Americans in the State and information regarding the
10 government services being utilized and underutilized by New Americans;
11 (2) a summary or survey of varying and successful approaches to
12 providing government services to New Americans in other states, with
13 particular focus on the topics of the role of education and training institutions,
14 professional licensing, housing, and support for employers and municipalities;
15 (3) in consultation with community-based organizations composed of or
16 serving New Americans, State agencies and departments that provided services
17 to New Americans, municipal government leaders, educational institutions,
18 and business leaders, information on:
19 (A) the existing State and local-level barriers for New Americans for
20 gainfully participating in the State’s workforce, economy, and business
21 communities;

1 (B) additional governmental services needed by New Americans but
2 not yet offered by the State; and
3 (C) the transfer or consolidation of existing governmental services
4 for New Americans that would be more efficiently provided by a new Office
5 of New Americans;
6 (4) the structure of a to-be-created independent Office of New
7 Americans; and
8 (5) the proposed structure, duties, funding, and labor needs of an Office
9 of New Americans.
10 (d) The Committee shall be composed of the following three
11 subcommittees, with duties and leadership to be assigned by the Chair at the
12 first meeting of the Committee:
13 (1) Legal Services;
14 (2) Equity and Access to State Programs and Resources; and
15 (3) Workforce, Economic Opportunity, and Inclusion.
16 (e) Assistance. The Committee shall have the administrative, technical,
17 and legal assistance of the Agency of Human Services.
18 (f) Date of Report. On or before September 1, 2026, the Committee shall
19 submit the written report required by subsection (c) of this section.
20 (g) Meetings.

1 (1) The Chair shall call the first meeting of the Committee to occur on
2 or before September 1, 2025.
3 (2) A majority of the membership shall constitute a quorum.
4 (3) The Committee shall cease to exist on the earlier of September 1,
5 2026 or the date that the Committee submits its written report.
6 (h) Compensation and reimbursement.
7 (1) For attendance at meetings during adjournment of the General
8 Assembly, a legislative member of the Committee serving in the member’s
9 capacity as a legislator shall be entitled to per diem compensation and
10 reimbursement of expenses pursuant to 2 V.S.A. § 23 for not more than 20
11 meetings. These payments shall be made from monies appropriated to the
12 General Assembly.
13 (2) Other members of the Committee shall be entitled to per diem
14 compensation and reimbursement of expenses as permitted under 32 V.S.A.
15 § 1010 for not more than 20 meetings. These payments shall be made from
16 monies appropriated to the Agency of Human Services.
17 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
18 This act shall take effect on passage.

(a) Creation. There is created the Office of New Americans Study
Committee to make recommendations for creating an independent Office of
New Americans.

(b) Membership. The Committee shall be composed of the following
members:
(1) the Director of the Vermont Refugee Office, who shall be Chair;
(2) one member, appointed by the Commissioner of Labor;
(3) one member, appointed by the Executive Director of the Office of
Racial Equity; and
(4) five members, appointed by the Governor, one who must be a New

Vermont;

Immigrants;

(D) one member, nominated by the Brattleboro Development Credit
Corporation; and
(E) one member, nominated by Migrant Justice.

report to the House Committees on Commerce and Economic Development and
on Government Operations and Military Affairs and the Senate Committees on
Economic Development, Housing and General Affairs and on Government
Operations concerning recommendations for creating an independent Office of
New Americans, including:
(1) a summary of the current demographic, economic, and public health
data regarding New Americans in the State and information regarding the
government services being utilized and underutilized by New Americans;
(2) a summary or survey of varying and successful approaches to
providing government services to New Americans in other states, with
particular focus on the topics of the role of education and training institutions,
professional licensing, housing, and support for employers and municipalities;
(3) in consultation with community-based organizations composed of or
serving New Americans, the Vermont Asylum Assistance Project, State
agencies and departments that provide services to New Americans, the
Secretary of State’s Office, municipal government leaders, educational
institutions, and business leaders, information on:
