5 Sec. 1. 21 V.S.A § 601 is amended to read:
6 § 601. DEFINITIONS
7 As used in this chapter:
8 * * *
9 (31) “Medical case management” means the planning and coordination
10 of health care services appropriate to achieve the goal of medical
11 rehabilitation.
12 (A) Medical case management may include medical case assessment,
13 including a personal interview with the injured employee; assistance in
14 developing, implementing, and coordinating a medical care plan with health
15 care providers in consultation with the injured employee and the employees’
16 family; and an evaluation of treatment results. The goal of medical case
17 management is to provide the injured employee with reasonable treatment
18 options to ensure that the injured employee can make an informed choice.
19 (B) Medical case managers shall not provide medical care or adjust
20 claims.

1 (C) An injured employee shall be entitled to medical case
2 management services if reasonably supported. Reasonable support includes a
3 recommendation made by a health care provider or evidence demonstrating the
4 injured employee’s medical recovery would benefit from the services, or both.

6 § 602. PROCESS AND PROCEDURE
7 * * *
8 (d) When an injured employee does not speak English fluently, the
9 employer shall pay for translation services to ensure the injured employee fully
10 understands the employee’s rights and can effectively participate in the
11 employee’s medical recovery and the workers’ compensation claims process.

18 durable medical equipment.
19 (2) “Services” means medical case management services.

4 subsection (e) of this section within 14 days after receiving a request, the
5 injured employee or the injured employee’s treating provider, if applicable,

3 weekly payment is mailed or deposited on or before the day established.
4 (B) Payment shall be made by direct deposit to a claimant who elects
5 that payment method. The employer shall notify the claimant of the claimant’s
6 right to payment by direct deposit.
7 (2) If the benefit payment is not mailed or deposited on the day
8 established, or if the payment is not mailed or deposited within five business

14 made after the established day; and

16 payments that are made after the established day.

18 the claimant’s mailing address or, in the case of direct deposit, transferred into
19 the designated account. In the event of a dispute, proof of payment shall be
20 established by affidavit.
