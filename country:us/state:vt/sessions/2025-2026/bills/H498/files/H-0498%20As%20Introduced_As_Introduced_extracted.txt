2 SHORT FORM
3 Introduced by Representative Headrick of Burlington
4 Referred to Committee on
5 Date:
6 Subject: Municipal and county government; regulatory provisions; police
7 power of municipalities; prohibition on criminalizing homelessness;
8 grants for municipalities to address homelessness; annual reporting
9 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to amend
10 various provisions of Title 24 to prohibit municipal corporations from adopting
11 civil or criminal ordinances that would prevent an individual from engaging in
12 life-sustaining activities on municipal property, such as sleeping, camping,
13 resting, or loitering. This bill also proposes to authorize municipal
14 corporations to adopt certain ordinances prohibiting the use of municipal
15 property for life-sustaining activities whenever adequate shelter is available.
16 This bill also proposes to establish a State-funded grant program to support
17 municipal diversionary programs for unhoused individuals; municipal services
18 relating to mental health care, addiction treatment, and job training; and
19 partnerships between municipalities and nonprofit organizations to provide
20 temporary, transitional, and permanent housing. This bill also proposes to
21 allow municipalities to designate humanitarian zones in public spaces, in
VT LEG #382131 v.1

1 which unhoused individuals may access sanitation facilities, water, and
2 temporary shelter. This bill also proposes to appropriate monies to the Agency
3 of Human Services for purposes of expanding housing programs that prioritize
4 immediate, low-barrier housing solutions for unhoused individuals and
5 families. This bill also proposes to amend statutes related to affordable
6 housing to provide tax incentives for developers that create affordable housing
7 units, enact rent stabilization and eviction protection for residential tenants,
8 appropriate monies for the construction of affordable housing units by the
9 State, and establish public-private partnerships to expand the availability of
10 housing stock statewide. This bill also proposes to amend the statutes
11 governing regional and municipal planning to require municipalities to include
12 homelessness strategies within the municipal plan and to authorize grants to be
13 used for purposes of technical assistance for municipalities that adopt and
14 revise new plans. This bill also proposes to require annual reports from
15 municipalities and State agencies concerning the status of homelessness within
16 the State, the effectiveness of any measures that have been implemented, and
17 progress towards the reduction of homelessness in Vermont. This bill also
18 proposes to establish a task force, composed of legislators, municipal
19 representatives, advocates, and individuals with lived experience, to study and
20 oversee the implementation of the proposals within this bill.
