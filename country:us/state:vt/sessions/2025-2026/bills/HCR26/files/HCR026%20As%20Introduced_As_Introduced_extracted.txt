Section 1:
State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 26
Offered by All Members of the House
Olympics rugby sevens bronze medal
Whereas, rugby is not the most commonly played team sport in the United States, but none is
more exhilarating or exciting, and no Vermonter or American is more adept on the rugby field
than Ilona Maher, and
Whereas, Ilona Maher graduated in 2014 from Burlington High School, where she
enthusiastically played basketball, field hockey, soccer, and softball, and she started her love
affair with rugby as a member of the South Burlington High School’s rugby football club, and
Whereas, initially attending Norwich University, the Quinnipiac University Bobcats rugby
coach recruited her, and for the next three years, she excelled as the Bobcats won three National
Intercollegiate Rugby Association (NIRA) women’s championships; in each of these years, the
NIRA named her to its All-American women’s team, and in 2017, the NIRA honored Ilona
Maher as the nation’s top collegiate women’s rugby player, and
Whereas, this Quinnipiac University nursing graduate and master’s degree graduate of DeVry
University debuted as a women’s USA Rugby World Cup Sevens team member in 2018 and
represented the United States in 2021 at the Tokyo Olympics, and even more exciting was her
2024 Paris Olympics appearance, where the U.S. women’s rugby sevens team won its first-ever
Olympic medal—a bronze—upon defeating Australia 14–12, and
Whereas, following her return from France, Ilona Maher was honored at an ebullient
welcome home ceremony in Burlington’s Battery Park, and she has used her rugby fame to
promote women’s sports and body positivity, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates Ilona Delsing Rosa Maher on her 2024 Paris
Olympics rugby sevens bronze medal, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Ilona
Maher.
