State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 82
and Harvey of Castleton
Offered by Senators Collamore, Hardy, Heffernan, Weeks, and Williams
House concurrent resolution congratulating the 2025 Fair Haven Union
High School Slaters State championship bowling team
Whereas, during the 2024–2025 season on the bowling lanes, the second-
seeded Fair Haven Slaters experienced its strongest challenge from the top-
ranked Randolph Union High Galloping Ghosts, and after defeating South
Burlington and Essexin the quarter-final and semifinal rounds, the Slaters
confronted their archrival for the State title at the Spare Time bowling facility
in Colchester, and
Whereas, despite losing the opening game to Randolph (172–150), the
Slaters immediately rebounded, winning the second (161–146), the third (243–
177), the fifth (217–168), and the sixth (233–164), and with four victories,
Fair Haven clinched the best-of-seven final round and won the 2025 State
bowling championship, and
Whereas, a caravan of over 20 cars greeted the victorious team in Castleton
and escorted the jubilant Slaters back to school, and
Whereas, the championship Slater bowlers were Wyatt DeLance, Aiden
Goyette, Nathan Bessette, Inshik Adams-Silva, Emma Eckler, Eli Donaldson,
Dylan Bania, Michael Towle, Korbin Hayes, George Stamp, Madison Stamp,
and Amberlyn Haley, and
Whereas, Head Coach Joe Donaldson and Assistant Coach Ron Carrrara
were superb guides for the team, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates the 2025 Fair Haven Union High
School Slaters State championship bowling team, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this
resolution to Fair Haven Union High School.
