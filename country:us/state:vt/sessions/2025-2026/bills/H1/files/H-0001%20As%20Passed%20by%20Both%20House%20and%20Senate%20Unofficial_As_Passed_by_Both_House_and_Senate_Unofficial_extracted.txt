4 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
5 Sec. 1. 3 V.S.A. § 1223 is amended to read:
6 § 1223. PROCEDURE FOR ACCEPTING AND REFERRING
7 COMPLAINTS
8 (a) Accepting complaints.
9 (1) On behalf of the Commission, the Executive Director shall accept
10 complaints from any source regarding governmental ethics in any of the three
11 branches of State government or of the State’s campaign finance law set forth
12 in 17 V.S.A. chapter 61.
13 * * *
14 (b) Preliminary review by Executive Director. The Executive Director
15 shall conduct a preliminary review of complaints made to the Commission in
16 order to take action as set forth in this subsection and section 1223a of this
17 title, which shall include referring complaints to all relevant entities, including
18 the Commission itself.
19 (1) Governmental conduct regulated by law.
20 * * *
21 (2) Department of Human Resources, Personnel Policy and Procedure
22 Manual.

1 * * *
2 (3) Campaign finance.
3 * * *
4 (4) Legislative and Judicial Branches; attorneys.
5 * * *
6 (5) Municipal Code of Ethics. If the complaint alleges a violation of the
7 Municipal Code of Ethics, the Executive Director shall refer the complaint to
8 the designated ethics liaison of the appropriate municipality.
9 (6) The Executive Director shall close any complaint that the Executive
10 Director does not refer as set forth in subdivisions (1)–(5) of this subsection.
11 (c) Consultation on unethical conduct Commission advice on the
12 application of the State Code of Ethics on referred complaints.
13 (1) If the Executive Director refers a complaint under subsection (b) of
14 this section, the Executive Director shall signify any likely unethical conduct
15 described in the complaint. Any entity receiving a referred complaint and,
16 except those for complaints alleging a violation of the Municipal Code of
17 Ethics as set forth in subdivision (b)(5) of this section, shall consult with the
18 Commission regarding the specify any application of the State Code of Ethics
19 to facts the allegations presented in the complaint and include a recommended
20 action. The consultation shall be in writing and occur within 60 days after an
21 entity receives a referred complaint and prior to the entity making a

1 Sec. 3. 3 V.S.A. § 1231 is amended to read:
2 § 1231. RECORDS; CONFIDENTIALITY
3 (a) Intent. It is the intent of this section both to protect the reputation of
4 public servants from public disclosure of frivolous complaints against them
5 and to fulfill the public’s right to know any unethical conduct committed by a
6 public servant that results in issued warnings, reprimands, or recommended
7 actions.
8 (b) Public records. Except as where otherwise provided in this chapter,
9 public records relating to the Commission’s handling of complaints, alleged
10 unethical conduct, investigations, proceedings, and executed resolution
11 agreements are exempt from public inspection and copying under the Public
12 Records Act and shall be kept confidential, except those public records
13 required or permitted to be released under this chapter. Records subject to
14 public inspection and copying under the Public Records Act shall include:
15 * * *
16 (6) any records, as determined by the Commission, that support a
17 warning, reprimand, recommendation, or summary of an executed resolution
18 agreement, including consultations created pursuant to subsection 1223(c) of
19 this title and investigation reports in accordance with subdivisions (1) and (2)
20 of this subsection.
