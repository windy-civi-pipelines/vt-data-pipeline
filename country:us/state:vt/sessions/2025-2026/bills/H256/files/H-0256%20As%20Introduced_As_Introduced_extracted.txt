1 H.256
2 Introduced by Representative Krasnow of South Burlington
3 Referred to Committee on
4 Date:
5 Subject: Executive; Judiciary Employees Labor Relations Act; Judiciary
6 supervisors
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to allow
8 Judiciary supervisors to organize and bargain collectively.
9 An act relating to Judiciary Employees Labor Relations Act
10 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
11 Sec. 1. 3 V.S.A. § 1011 is amended to read:
12 § 1011. DEFINITIONS
13 As used in this chapter:
14 * * *
15 (8) “Employee,” “Employee” means any individual employed and
16 compensated on a permanent or limited status basis by the Judiciary
17 Department, including permanent part-time employees and any individual
18 whose employment has ceased as a consequence of, or in connection with, any
19 current labor dispute or because of an unfair labor practice. “Employee” does
20 not include any of the following:
VT LEG #380565 v.1
BILL AS INTRODUCED H.256
2025 Page 2 of 2
1 (A) a Justice, judge, assistant judge, magistrate, or hearing officer;
2 (B) the Court Administrator;
3 (C) a managerial, supervisory, or confidential employee;
4 (D) a law clerk, attorney, or administrative assistant or private
5 secretary to a judge, Justice, or Court Administrator;
6 (E) an individual employed on a temporary, contractual, seasonal, or
7 on-call basis, including an intern;
8 (F) an employee during the initial or extended probationary period;
9 (G) the head of a department or division;
10 (H) [Repealed.]
11 (I) an attorney for the Supreme Court, for the Court Administrator, or
12 for any board or commission created by the Supreme Court;
13 (J) an employee paid by the State who is appointed part-time as
14 county clerk pursuant to 4 V.S.A. § 651 or 691; or
15 (K) an employee who, after hearing by the Board upon petition of
16 any individual, the employer, or a collective bargaining unit, is determined to
17 be in a position that is sufficiently inconsistent with the spirit and intent of this
18 chapter to warrant exclusion.
19 * * *
20 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
21 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
