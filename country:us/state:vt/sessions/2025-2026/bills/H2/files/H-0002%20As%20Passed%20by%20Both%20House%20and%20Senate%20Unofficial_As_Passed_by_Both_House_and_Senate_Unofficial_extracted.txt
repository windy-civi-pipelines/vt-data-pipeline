4 * * * Family Division Delinquency Jurisdiction * * *
5 Sec. 1. 33 V.S.A. § 5102 is amended to read:
6 § 5102. DEFINITIONS AND PROVISIONS OF GENERAL APPLICATION
7 As used in the juvenile judicial proceedings chapters:
8 * * *
9 (2) “Child” means any of the following:
10 * * *
11 (C) an individual who has been alleged to have committed or has
12 committed an act of delinquency after becoming 10 12 years of age and prior
13 to becoming 22 years of age, unless otherwise provided in chapter 52 or 52A
14 of this title; provided, however:
15 (i) that an individual who is alleged to have committed an act
16 before attaining 10 years of age that would be murder as defined in 13 V.S.A.
17 § 2301 if committed by an adult may be subject to delinquency proceedings;
18 and
19 (ii), that an individual may be considered a child for the period of
20 time the court retains jurisdiction under section 5104 of this title.

2 Sec. 2. 33 V.S.A. § 5103(c) is amended to read:
3 (c)(1) Except as otherwise provided by this title and by subdivision (2) of
4 this subsection, jurisdiction over a child shall not be extended beyond the
5 child’s 18th birthday.
6 (2)(A) Jurisdiction over a child with a delinquency may be extended
7 until six months beyond the child’s:
8 (i) 19th 20th birthday if the child was 16 or 17 years of age when
9 he or she the child committed the offense; or
10 (ii) 20th 21st birthday if the child was 18 years of age when he or
11 she the child committed the offense.

7 any offense other than those specified in subsection 5204(a) of this title or
8 subdivision (c)(2) or (3) of this section before attaining 19 20 years of age shall

12 § 5203. TRANSFER FROM OTHER COURTS
13 (a) If it appears to a Criminal Division of the Superior Court that the
14 defendant was under 19 20 years of age at the time the offense charged was
15 alleged to have been committed and the offense charged is an offense not
16 specified in subsection 5204(a) or subdivision 5201(c)(2) or (3) of this title,
17 that court shall forthwith transfer the proceeding to the Family Division of the
18 Superior Court under the authority of this chapter, and the minor shall then be
19 considered to be subject to this chapter as a child charged with a delinquent
20 act.

1 Sec. 10. AGENCY OF HUMAN SERVICES PROGRESS REPORTS
2 (a) On or before July 1, 2026 and December 1, 2026, the Agency of Human
3 Services shall report to the Joint Legislative Justice Oversight Committee, the
4 Senate and House Committees on Judiciary, the House Committee on
5 Corrections and Institutions, the Senate Committee on Institutions, the House
6 Committee on Human Services, and the Senate Committee on Health and
7 Welfare on its progress toward implementing the requirement of this act that
8 the Raise the Age initiative take effect on July 1, 2027. The progress reports
9 required by this section shall describe progress toward implementation of the
10 Raise the Age initiative, as measured by qualitative and quantitative data
11 related to the following priorities:
12 (1) establishing a secure residential facility;
13 (2) expanding capacity for nonresidential treatment programs to provide
14 community-based services;
15 (3) ensuring that residential treatment programs are used appropriately
16 and to their full potential;
17 (4) expanding capacity for Balanced and Restorative Justice (BARJ)
18 contracts;
19 (5) expanding capacity for the provision of services to children with
20 developmental disabilities;
VT LEG #382148 v.1

2025 Page 7 of 8
1 (6) establishing a stabilization program for children who are
2 experiencing a mental health crisis;
3 (7) enhancing long-term treatment for children;
4 (8) programming to help children, particularly 18- and 19-year-olds,
5 transition to adulthood;
6 (9) developing district-specific data and information on family services
7 workforce development, including turnover, retention, and vacancy rates; times
8 needed to fill open positions; training opportunities and needs; and instituting a
9 positive culture for employees;
10 (10) installation of a comprehensive child welfare information system;
11 and
12 (11) plans for and measures taken to secure funding for the goals listed
13 in this section.
14 (b) The report required by this section shall provide utilization data for the
15 Red Clover Treatment Facility, including how many youths utilize the Facility
16 on a monthly and annual basis, the length of stay, the treatment needs of the
17 youths who are placed at the Facility, racial and gender demographic data for
18 youths who are placed at the Facility, and any other data deemed relevant by
19 the Department.

1 (c) Failure to meet one or more of the progress report elements listed in
2 subsection (a) of this section shall not be a basis for extending the
3 implementation of the Raise the Age initiative beyond July 1, 2027.
4 * * * Effective Dates * * *
5 Sec. 11. EFFECTIVE DATES; APPLICABILITY
6 (a) Secs. 1, 2, and 10 shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
7 (b) Secs. 3 and 4 and this section shall take effect on March 31, 2025.
8 (c) Secs. 5–9 shall take effect on July 1, 2027.
