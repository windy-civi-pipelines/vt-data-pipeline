1 S.37
2 Introduced by Senators Clarkson, Ram Hinsdale and White
3 Referred to Committee on
4 Date:
5 Subject: Labor; unemployment compensation; eligibility; employment; partial
6 unemployment benefits
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to provide that
8 wages earned for work that would not qualify an individual to receive
9 unemployment insurance benefits shall not be counted when determining an
10 individual’s weekly unemployment insurance benefit amount, and to make
11 individuals who work for an educational institution in any capacity other than
12 an instructional, research, or principal administrative capacity eligible for
13 unemployment insurance between academic terms.
14 An act relating to unemployment insurance eligibility and benefits
15 Sec. 1. 21 V.S.A. § 1338a is amended to read:
16 § 1338a. DISREGARDED EARNINGS
17 (a) An individual shall be deemed “partially unemployed” in any week of
18 less than full-time work if the wages earned by the individual with respect to
19 such the week are less than the weekly benefit amount the individual would be
20 entitled to receive if totally unemployed and eligible. As used in For purposes

1 of this section, “wages” in any one week includes only that amount of
2 remuneration earned by the individual in employment, rounded to the nearest
3 dollar that is in excess of 50 percent of the individual’s weekly wage wages in
4 employment.
5 * * *
6 Sec. 2. 21 V.S.A. § 1343 is amended to read:
7 § 1343. CONDITIONS
8 * * *
9 (c) Benefits are payable on the basis of service in employment as defined in
10 subdivisions 1301(6)(A)(ix) and (x) of this subchapter, in the same amount, on
11 the same terms, and subject to the same conditions as benefits payable on the
12 basis of other service subject to this chapter, except that:
13 (1) With respect to services performed in an instructional, research, or
14 principal administrative capacity for an educational institution, benefits shall
15 not be payable on the basis of such the services for any week of unemployment
16 commencing during the period between two successive academic years or
17 terms (or, when an agreement provides instead for a similar period between
18 two regular but not successive terms, during such period) or during a period of
19 paid sabbatical leave provided for in the individual’s contract, to any
20 individual if the individual performs such the services in the first of the
21 academic years or terms and if there is a contract or reasonable assurance that

1 the individual will perform services in any such capacity for any educational
2 institution in the second of the academic years or terms.
3 (2) With respect to services performed in any other capacity for an
4 educational institution, benefits shall not be payable on the basis of such
5 services to any individual for any week of unemployment that commences
6 during a period between two successive academic years or terms if the
7 individual performs such services in the first of the academic years or terms
8 and there is a reasonable assurance that the individual will perform such
9 services for any educational institution in the second of the academic years or
10 terms, except that if benefits are denied to any individual under this
11 subdivision and the individual was not offered an opportunity to perform such
12 services for the educational institution for the second of the academic years or
13 terms, the individual shall be entitled to a retroactive payment of the benefits
14 for each week for which the individual filed a timely claim for benefits and for
15 which benefits were denied solely by reason of this subdivision. [Repealed.]
16 (3) With respect to any services described in subdivision (1) or (2) of
17 this subsection, benefits shall not be payable on the basis of services in any
18 such capacities to any individual for any week that commences during an
19 established and customary vacation period or holiday recess if the individual
20 performs such the services in the period immediately before the vacation
21 period or holiday recess, and there is a reasonable assurance that the individual
VT LEG #380117 v.1

2025 Page 4 of 4
1 will perform such the services in the period immediately following the
2 vacation period or holiday recess.
3 (4) With respect to any services described in subdivision (1) or (2) of
4 this subsection, benefits shall not be payable on the basis of services in any
5 such capacities as specified in subdivisions (1), (2), and (3) of this subsection
6 to any individual who performed such the services in an educational institution
7 while in the employ of an educational service agency. As used in this
8 subdivision, the term “educational service agency” means a governmental
9 agency or governmental entity that is established and operated exclusively for
10 the purpose of providing such services to one or more educational institutions.
11 * * *
12 Sec. 3. EFFECTIVE DATE
13 This act shall take effect on passage.
