1 § 7606. EFFECT OF EXPUNGEMENT
2 (a) Order and notice. Upon finding that the requirements for expungement
3 have been met, the court shall issue an order that shall include provisions that
4 its effect is to annul the record of the arrest, conviction, and sentence and that
5 such person shall be treated in all respects as if the person had never been
6 arrested, convicted, or sentenced for the offense. The court shall provide
7 notice of the expungement to the respondent, Vermont Crime Information
8 Center (VCIC), the arresting agency, the Restitution Unit of the Vermont
9 Center for Crime Victim Services, and any other entity that may have a record
10 related to the order to expunge. The VCIC shall provide notice of the
11 expungement to the Federal Bureau of Investigation’s National Crime
12 Information Center.
13 (b) Effect.
14 (1) Upon entry of an expungement order, the order shall be legally
15 effective immediately and the person whose record is expunged shall be treated
16 in all respects as if he or she the person had never been arrested, convicted, or
17 sentenced for the offense.
18 (2) In any application for employment, license, or civil right or privilege
19 or in an appearance as a witness in any proceeding or hearing, a person may be
20 required to answer questions about a previous criminal history record only with
21 respect to arrests or convictions that have not been expunged.
