Section 1:
State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 150
Nigro of Bennington
Offered by Senators Bongartz and Plunkett
House concurrent resolution congratulating Bennington Lodge No. 567 of the Benevolent and Protective
Order of Elks on 125 years of community service and good fellowship
Whereas, Charles Algernon Sidney Vivian inspired the establishment of the Benevolent and Protective Order
of Elks (the Elks), a momentous event that occurred in New York City on February 16, 1868, and
Whereas, the purpose of this fraternal organization is “[t]o promote and practice the four cardinal virtues of
Charity, Justice, Brotherly Love and Fidelity; to promote the welfare and enhance the happiness of its members;
to quicken the spirit of American Patriotism and cultivate good fellowship,” and
Whereas, Elks District Deputy P.M. Meldon of Rutland formed Bennington Lodge No. 567 (the Lodge) on
July 10, 1900, and John S. Brant served as the first Exalted Ruler of the Lodge until 1902, and
Whereas, originally located at the 300 block of Main Street, in 1912, the Lodge moved to the third floor of
the Richie Building at 469 Main Street, and ultimately, in 1945, the Lodge purchased the historic Winslow
Home on Washington Avenue, which today remains the Lodge’s headquarters, and
Whereas, the Lodge performs the central charitable prong of the Elks’ mission through involvement in
activities supporting youth (Boy Scouts, Girl Scouts, Little League Baseball, the Hoop Shoot basketball
competition, awarding scholarships totaling $19,000.00 in 2024, sponsoring the Americanism Essay Contest,
and promoting drug awareness programs); assisting veterans (constructing a fishing dock, conducting a fishing
derby, hosting a Las Vegas Night, sponsoring Christmas events, and hosting dining and entertainment
activities); participating in the Elks’ State Project (operating Silver Towers Camp in Ripton for children and
adults with special needs); and offering direct community support (by providing Thanksgiving meals and
spearheading foster family projects), and
Whereas, the 125th anniversary of the Lodge finds the organization thriving, boasting a growing
membership of over 800 enthusiastic Elks, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates Bennington Lodge No. 567 of the Benevolent and Protective Order
of Elks on 125 years of community service and good fellowship, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to Bennington Lodge No.
567 of the Benevolent Order of Elks.
