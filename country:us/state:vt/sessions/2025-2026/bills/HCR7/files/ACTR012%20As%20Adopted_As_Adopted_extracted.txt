2025
High School Slaters Division II championship boys’ football team.
(H.C.R.7)
Offered by Representatives Canfield of Fair Haven, Casey of Hubbardton, and Harvey
of Castleton
Offered by Senators Collamore, Hardy, Heffernan, Weeks and Williams
Whereas, the 2024 third-seeded Slaters were pleased to reach the season’s decisive
game against the top-seeded Green Knights of Rice Memorial High School, and
Whereas, in this high-scoring gridiron contest, played in St. Johnsbury, Rice assumed
a 12–7 first-quarter lead that sparked Fair Haven’s football prowess, resulting in a
Slaters’ 21–15 halftime lead, and the second half, despite Rice’s attempted comeback,
concluded in a 35–22 Fair Haven triumph, as the team clinched the school’s first
divisional football championship since 2017, and
Whereas, the victorious Slaters were Jack Almeida, Bryson Kelly, Mason Gutel, Tylor
Jackson, Evan Spanos, Logan Knipes, Derek Webb, Sam Kyhill, Phil Bean, Cody
Adams, Devin LaFrancois, Adam Williams, Konner Savage, Easton Adams, Aydin Long,
Jacob Torrey, Connor LaFrancois, Aden Wilkins, Caleb Long, Max Kyhill, Anthony
Szabo, Cole Belden, Jonathan Hutchins, Alex Messer, Aiden Goyette, Tommy Buxton,
Colbi Clark, Caden Lynch, Seth Czachor, Jackson Beckwith, Aiden St. Armour, Tanner
Raymond, Porter Silva, Gabe Barrows, Ty Ferguson, Jeremiah Kirby, Gabe Bache, Gavin
Spencer, Austin Williams, Lamont Jones, and Jacob Jamieson, and
Whereas, Head Coach Adam Perry and assistant coaches Keith Hier, PJ Szabo, Hunter
Muzzy, Chris Parker, Joel Perry, Caleb Lisai, and DJ Perry were great contributors to the
Slaters’ achievement, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates the 2024 Fair Haven Union High School
Slaters Division II championship boys’ football team, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this resolution to
Fair Haven Union High School.
VT LEG #382024 v.1
