4 Subject: Agriculture; fertilizer; beneficial substances; miscellaneous subjects
5 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to amend the
6 State statutes on the use of plant amendments, plant biostimulants, and soil
7 amendments to be consistent with the uniform standards for regulation of
8 beneficial substances. The bill would define “beneficial substances” to mean
9 any substance or compound, other than primary, secondary, and micro plant
10 nutrients (fertilizers), and excluding pesticides, that can be demonstrated by
11 scientific research to be beneficial to one or more species of plants, soil, or
12 media.

3 § 364. REGISTRATION
4 (a) Each brand or grade or formula of fertilizer, plant amendment, plant
5 biostimulant, or soil amendment beneficial substance shall be registered in the
6 name of the person whose name appears upon the label before being
7 distributed in this State. The application for registration shall be submitted to
8 the Secretary on a form furnished by the Agency of Agriculture, Food and
9 Markets and shall be accompanied by a fee of $85.00 per grade or formulation
10 registered. Upon approval by the Secretary, a copy of the registration shall be
11 furnished to the applicant. All registrations expire on December 31 of each
12 year. The application shall include the following information:
13 (1) the brand and grade or formulation;
14 (2) the guaranteed analysis if applicable; and
15 (3) the name and address of the registrant.

17 amendment, plant biostimulant, or soil amendment or beneficial substance that
18 is already registered under this chapter by another person, provided there is no
19 change in the label for the fertilizer, plant amendment, plant biostimulant, or
20 soil amendment or beneficial substance.

19 (1) its labeling is false or misleading in any particular; or

1 § 369. ADULTERATION
2 No person shall distribute an adulterated lime, plant amendment, plant
3 biostimulant, soil amendment beneficial substance, or fertilizer product. A
4 fertilizer, plant amendment, plant biostimulant, soil amendment beneficial
5 substance, or lime shall be deemed to be adulterated if:
6 (1) it contains any deleterious or harmful ingredient in an amount

8 aquatic life, soil, or water when applied in accordance with directions for use
9 on the label, or if uses of the product may result in contamination or
10 condemnation of a raw agricultural commodity by use, or if adequate warning
11 statements or directions for use that may be necessary to protect plant life,
12 animals, humans, aquatic life, soil, or water are not shown on the label;
13 (2) its composition falls below or differs from that which it is purported
14 to possess by its labeling;
15 (3) it contains crop seed or weed seed; or
16 (4) it contains heavy metals, radioactive substances, or synthetic
17 organics in amounts sufficient to render it injurious to livestock or human
18 health when applied in accordance with directions for use on the label, or if
19 adequate warning statements or directions for use that may be necessary to
20 protect livestock or human health are not shown on the label.
