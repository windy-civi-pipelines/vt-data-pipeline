1 S.89
2 Introduced by Senator Vyhovsky
3 Referred to Committee on
4 Date:
5 Subject: Public safety; survivorsʼ benefits
6 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to extend
7 survivors’ benefits to survivors of law enforcement officers, Department of
8 Corrections employees, Family Services Division employees, and employees
9 in State-operated therapeutic community residences or inpatient psychiatric
10 hospital units who die while in the line of duty or from an occupation-related
11 illness.
12 An act relating to expanding survivor benefits
13 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
14 Sec. 1. SHORT TITLE
15 This act may be cited as “Jessica’s Law.”
16 Sec. 2. 20 V.S.A. § 3171 is amended to read:
17 § 3171. DEFINITIONS
18 As used in this chapter:
19 * * *

1 (3) “Emergency personnel” means:
2 (A) firefighters as defined in subdivision 3151(3) of this title; and
3 (B) emergency medical personnel and volunteer personnel as defined
4 in 24 V.S.A. § 2651;
5 (C) law enforcement officers who have been certified by the Vermont
6 Criminal Justice Council pursuant to section 2358 of this title;
7 (D) facility employees of the Department of Corrections and
8 Department of Corrections employees who provide direct security and
9 treatment services to offenders under supervision in the community;
10 (E) classified employees of the Family Services Division of the
11 Department for Children and Families; and
12 (F) classified employees of State-operated therapeutic community
13 residences or inpatient psychiatric hospital units.
14 (4) “Line of duty” means:
15 (A) answering or returning from with respect to firefighters,
16 emergency medical personnel, and volunteer personnel:
17 (i) service in answer to a call of the department or service for a
18 fire or emergency or, including going to and returning from a fire or
19 emergency or participating in a fire or emergency training drill; or
20 (B)(ii) similar service in another town or district to which the
21 department or service has been called for firefighting or emergency purposes.;
VT LEG #380749 v.2

1 (B) with respect to law enforcement officers:
2 (i) service as a law enforcement officer in answer to a complaint
3 lodged with the department or in response to a disorder, including going to,
4 returning from, and investigating or responding to the complaint or disorder; or
5 (ii) service under orders from the department or in any emergency
6 for which the law enforcement officer serves as a law enforcement officer;
7 (C) with respect to covered employees of the Department of
8 Corrections, discharging their duties as employees;
9 (D) with respect to classified employees of the Family Services
10 Division of the Department for Children and Families, discharging their duties
11 as employees; and
12 (E) with respect to classified employees of State-operated therapeutic
13 community residences or inpatient psychiatric hospital units, discharging their
14 duties as employees.
15 * * *
16 Sec. 3. 20 V.S.A. § 3172 is amended to read:
17 § 3172. EMERGENCY PERSONNEL SURVIVORS BENEFIT REVIEW
18 BOARD
19 * * *
20 (c) If the Board decides to award a monetary benefit, the benefit shall be
21 paid to the surviving spouse or, if the emergency personnel had no spouse at

1 the time of death, to the surviving child, or equally among surviving children.
2 If the deceased emergency personnel is not survived by a spouse or child, the
3 benefit shall be paid to a surviving parent, or equally between surviving
4 parents. If the deceased emergency personnel is not survived by a spouse,
5 children, or parents, the Board shall not award a monetary benefit under this
6 chapter.
7 (d) Upon a Board decision to award a monetary benefit under this chapter,
8 the Treasurer shall make payment to the beneficiaries as described in
9 subsection (c) of this section. The Treasurer shall have up to one year from the
10 date of receiving the claim to disburse the funds.
11 * * *
12 Sec. 4. EFFECTIVE DATE
13 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
