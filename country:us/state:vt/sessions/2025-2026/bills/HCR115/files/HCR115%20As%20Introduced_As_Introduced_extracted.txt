Section 1:
State of Vermont
House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 115
Offered by Representative Burtt of Danville
Offered by Senator Beck
Bears Division IV championship boys’ basketball team
Whereas, the third-seeded Danville Bears experienced a variety of winning
outcomes on their journey to the Division IV championship game against the
top-seeded West Rutland Golden Horde; having overwhelmed 14th-seeded
Rivendell Academy 102–38 in the opening game, easily toppling 11th-ranked
Long Trail School 71–31 in the quarterfinal contest, and, in the thrilling
semifinal round, narrowly defeating neighboring second-seeded
Twinfield/Cabot 48–47, and
Whereas, at the decisive game played at the Barre Auditorium, Danville
jumped in front quickly 10–2, was comfortably ahead at halftime 31–18, and
clinched the school’s first boys’ basketball crown, playing under their new
mascot name, “the Bears,” 55–42, with one teammate scoring an impressive
seven three-point plays, and
Whereas, the triumphant Bears (20–4) were Kohl Guinard, Sebastian
Eldred, Andrew Joncas, Ben Barber, Connor Winn, Samson Kitonga, Alijah
Andrews, Landen Fournier, Arius Andrews, Jackson Giroux, and Keegan
Wheeler, and
Whereas, Head Coach Jason Brigham; assistant coaches Robert Rome, Matt
Colburn, and Cooper Calkins; and Athletic Trainer Ryan Farley supported the
team expertly and effectively, now therefore be it
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives:
That the General Assembly congratulates the 2025 Danville High School
Bears Division IV championship boys’ basketball team, and be it further
Resolved: That the Secretary of State be directed to send a copy of this
resolution to Danville High School.

House of Representatives
Montpelier, Vermont
Concurrent House Resolution
H.C.R. 115
Offered by Representative Burtt of Danville
Offered by Senator Beck
House concurrent resolution congratulating the 2025 Danville High School
Bears Division IV championship boys’ basketball team
Whereas, the third-seeded Danville Bears experienced a variety of winning
outcomes on their journey to the Division IV championship game against the
top-seeded West Rutland Golden Horde; having overwhelmed 14th-seeded
Rivendell Academy 102–38 in the opening game, easily toppling 11th-ranked
Long Trail School 71–31 in the quarterfinal contest, and, in the thrilling
semifinal round, narrowly defeating neighboring second-seeded
Twinfield/Cabot 48–47, and
Whereas, at the decisive game played at the Barre Auditorium, Danville
jumped in front quickly 10–2, was comfortably ahead at halftime 31–18, and
clinched the school’s first boys’ basketball crown, playing under their new
mascot name, “the Bears,” 55–42, with one teammate scoring an impressive
seven three-point plays, and
Whereas, the triumphant Bears (20–4) were Kohl Guinard, Sebastian
Eldred, Andrew Joncas, Ben Barber, Connor Winn, Samson Kitonga, Alijah
Andrews, Landen Fournier, Arius Andrews, Jackson Giroux, and Keegan
Wheeler, and

resolution to Danville High School.
