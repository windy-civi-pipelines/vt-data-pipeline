5 Subject: Motor vehicles; operator’s licenses; renewal; endorsements; older

7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to require
8 residents 70 years of age or older to appear in person and pass a vision test to
9 renew an operator’s license and to require an endorsement to operate a motor
10 home weighing at least 10,000 pounds and not more than 26,000 pounds and a
11 commercial driver’s license to operate a motor home weighing at least 26,001
12 pounds.

14 (a) The Department shall establish standards for and shall administer the
15 Motor Home Operator Training Program. The Program shall include operator
16 training courses and instructor training. The Department may expand the
17 Program to include public awareness, alcohol and drug effects, motor home
18 operator improvement, licensing improvement, Program promotion, or other
19 motor home safety programs.

6 throughout the State, support and implement Program and funding guidelines,
7 and supervise instructors and other personnel as necessary. The training
8 specialist may be a trained chief instructor.

10 provided to meet the reasonably anticipated needs of all persons in the State
11 who desire to participate in the Program. The Department shall issue

16 driving proficiency, and the necessary aptitude for instructing students.
17 (b) The Commissioner shall establish minimum requirements for the
18 qualifications of a motor home operator training instructor. The minimum
19 requirements shall include the following:

2 operator’s license with a motor home endorsement or a valid commercial

5 any time during the preceding two years.

16 the classified service established pursuant to 3 V.S.A. chapter 13.
