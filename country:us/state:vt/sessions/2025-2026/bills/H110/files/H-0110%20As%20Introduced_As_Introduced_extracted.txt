1 H.110
2 Introduced by Representative Boyden of Cambridge
3 Referred to Committee on
4 Date:
5 Subject: Alcoholic beverages; licenses; malt beverage consumer shipping
6 licenses; reciprocity requirement
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to limit the
8 issuance of out-of-state malt beverage consumer shipping licenses to breweries
9 that are located in states that allow Vermont brewers to acquire consumer
10 shipping licenses.
11 An act relating to a reciprocity requirement for out-of-state consumer
12 shipping licenses
13 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
14 Sec. 1. 7 V.S.A. § 277 is amended to read:
15 § 277. MALT, VINOUS, AND READY-TO-DRINK SPIRITS BEVERAGE
16 CONSUMER SHIPPING LICENSE
17 * * *
18 (b)(1) A manufacturer or rectifier of malt beverages, vinous beverages, or
19 ready-to-drink spirits beverages licensed in another state that operates a
20 brewery, winery, or distillery in the United States and holds valid state and
VT LEG #379954 v.1
BILL AS INTRODUCED H.110
2025 Page 2 of 2
1 federal permits and licenses may be granted an out-of-state consumer shipping
2 license by filing with the Division of Liquor Control an application in a form
3 required by the Commissioner accompanied by copies of the applicant’s
4 current out-of-state manufacturer’s license and the fee provided in section 204
5 of this title.
6 (2) A manufacturer or rectifier of malt beverages may be granted an out-
7 of-state consumer shipping license only if the manufacturer or rectifier holds a
8 license and operates a brewery in a state that allows Vermont manufacturers or
9 rectifiers to ship malt beverages to consumers in that state.
10 (3) An out-of-state consumer shipping license may be renewed annually
11 by submitting to the Division the fee provided in section 204 of this title
12 accompanied by the licensee’s current out-of-state manufacturer’s license.
13 (3)(4) As used in this section, “out-of-state” means any state other than
14 Vermont, any territory or possession of the United States, and does not include
15 a foreign country.
16 * * *
17 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
18 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
