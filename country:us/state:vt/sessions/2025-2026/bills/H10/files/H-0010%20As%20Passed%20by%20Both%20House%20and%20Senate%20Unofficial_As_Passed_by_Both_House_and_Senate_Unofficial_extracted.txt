1 H.10
3 Barre
4 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
5 Sec. 1. CHARTER AMENDMENT APPROVAL
6 The General Assembly approves the amendments to the charter of the City
7 of Barre as set forth in this act. The voters of the City approved the
8 amendments on May 14, 2024.
9 Sec. 2. 24 App. V.S.A. chapter 1 is amended to read:
10 CHAPTER 1. CITY OF BARRE
11 * * *
12 § 110. FISCAL YEAR
13 The fiscal year of the City and the City School District shall begin on the
14 first day of July and end on the last day of June of each calendar year. The
15 fiscal year shall constitute the budget and accounting year as used in this
16 charter.
17 § 111. BONDING OF CITY OFFICIALS
18 The Mayor, councilors, members of the Police Department, City Manager,
19 Finance Director, Superintendent of Public Works, Tax Collector, and Clerk,
20 and Treasurer shall annually be bonded by the City for the faithful discharge of
21 their respective duties, as provided by State statute, and the expense of said
22 bonds to be paid by the City.

1 * * *
2 § 202. TIME OF ELECTIONS AND MEETINGS
3 (a) The annual City meeting of the City for the election of officers, the
4 voting of budgets, and any other business included in the warnings for the
5 meeting, shall be held on the first second Tuesday in March May, unless
6 otherwise legally warned by the Council.
7 * * *
8 § 203. SPECIAL CITY AND SCHOOL DISTRICT MEETINGS
9 Special City meetings and Special School District meetings shall be called
10 in the manner provided by the laws of the State, and the voting on all questions
11 shall be by the Australian ballot system, except for budget votes as provided in
12 section 207(d).
13 * * *
14 § 205. OFFICERS ELECTED
15 (a)(1) The legal voters shall elect biennially a Mayor and one person to
16 serve as a Clerk and Treasurer.
17 * * *
18 (3) Annually, the legal voters shall elect school commissioners to serve
19 for a term of three years, as follows:
20 (A) at the 2019 annual City meeting, and each successive three-year
21 cycle after that, three school commissioners;

1 (B) at the 2020 annual City meeting, and each successive three-year
2 cycle after that, two school commissioners;
3 (C) at the 2021 annual City meeting, and each successive three-year
4 cycle after that, two school commissioners. [Repealed.]
5 (b) The legal voters shall elect annually Spaulding Union High School
6 District School Board members in the manner, number, and term limit as is
7 specified in the Barre City/Barre Town Union High School Agreement, dated
8 November 30, 1986, or any such successor document. [Repealed.]
9 § 206. VACANCIES
10 (a) In case of vacancy of any elected municipal City officer, except a
11 councilor or school commissioner, occasioned by death, removal from the
12 City, resignation, or inability to serve, such vacancy, unless herein otherwise
13 provided, shall be filled by appointment by the City Council until the next
14 annual election. In such case, nomination may be made by any member for the
15 Council.
16 * * *
17 (c) In case of a vacancy of any school commissioner, occasioned by death,
18 removal from the City, resignation, or inability to serve, such vacancy, unless
19 herein otherwise provided, shall be filled by appointment by the remaining
20 members of the School Board until the next annual election. In such case,
21 nomination may be made by any member of the School Board. [Repealed.]

1 § 207. VOTING ON REQUEST OF THE CITY MANAGER
2 (a) Annually, on the first second Tuesday in March May, the legal voters of
3 the City shall meet to vote on the requests of the City Manager.
4 (b) If, at the annual March meeting, the voters disapprove the request of the
5 City Manager, the Mayor, or in his or her absence or disability, the City Clerk,
6 shall warn a general meeting of the legal voters to consider the article
7 disapproved by the voters and to authorize a specific sum of money therefor.
8 The meeting shall be in accordance with 17 V.S.A. §§ 2630–2689 and shall be
9 held at the same location as the previous meeting with the polls open for the
10 same hours as the previous meeting. If the voters continue to disapprove any
11 portion of the request, similar meetings shall be held in the same manner.
12 * * *
13 § 301. GOVERNING BODY
14 The City shall be governed by an elected Council and by an appointed City
15 Manager and by such other officers and employees as may be duly appointed
16 pursuant to this charter, City ordinances, or general laws of the State of
17 Vermont.
18 * * *
19 (2) Within the limitations of the foregoing, the Council shall have the
20 power to:
21 * * *

1 (B) appoint and remove a Treasurer, and such Assistant Treasurers as
2 shall be deemed necessary, who shall be responsible for the performance of all

4 (C) assign additional duties to offices, commissions, or departments
5 established by this charter, but may not discontinue or assign to any other
6 office, commission, or department duties assigned to a particular office,
7 commission, or department established by this charter;
8 (C)(D) make, amend, and repeal ordinances; and
9 (D)(E) adopt an official seal of the City.
10 * * *
11 § 315. COMPENSATION OF CITY OFFICIALS
12 Compensation of Mayor, councilors, and other appointees and City officers:

14 (3) When the legal voters have authorized an annual City and school
15 budget, the City Council, School Board, and/or or the City Manager shall not
16 authorize appropriations, expenditures, or payment in excess of the amount
17 voted. Deficit spending shall be prohibited by the City of Barre and the City of
18 Barre Schools.
