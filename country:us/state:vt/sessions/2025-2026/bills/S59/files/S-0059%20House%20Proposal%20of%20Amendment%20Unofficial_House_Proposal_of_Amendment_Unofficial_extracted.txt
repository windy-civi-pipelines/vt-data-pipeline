1 (c)(1) The time and place of all regular meetings subject to this section
2 shall be clearly designated by statute, charter, regulation, ordinance, bylaw,
3 resolution, or other determining authority of the public body, and this
4 information shall be available to any person upon request. The time and place
5 of all public hearings and meetings scheduled by all Executive Branch State
6 agencies, departments, boards, or commissions shall be available to the public
7 as required under 3 V.S.A. § 2222(c).
8 (2) The time, place, and purpose of a special meeting subject to this
9 section shall be publicly announced at least 24 hours before the meeting.
10 Municipal public bodies shall post notices of special meetings in or near the
11 municipal clerk’s office and in at least two other designated public places in
12 the municipality or a neighboring municipality, at least 24 hours before the
13 meeting. In addition, notice shall be given, either orally or in writing, to each
14 member of the public body at least 24 hours before the meeting, except that a
15 member may waive notice of a special meeting.

18 prior to a special meeting, a meeting agenda shall be:

5 Sec. 3. 1 V.S.A. § 313 is amended to read:
6 § 313. EXECUTIVE SESSIONS
7 (a) No public body may hold or conclude an executive session from which
8 the public is excluded, except by the affirmative vote of two-thirds of its
9 members present in the case of any public body of State government or of a
10 majority of its members present in the case of any public body of a
11 municipality or other political subdivision. A motion to go into executive
12 session shall indicate the nature of the business of the executive session, and
13 no other matter may be considered in the executive session. Such The vote to
14 enter executive session shall be taken in the course of an open meeting and the
15 result of the vote recorded in the minutes. No formal or binding action shall be
16 taken in executive session except for actions relating to the securing of real
17 estate options under subdivision (2) of this subsection. Minutes of an
18 executive session need not be taken, but if they are, the minutes shall,
19 notwithstanding subsection 312(b) of this title, be exempt from public copying
20 and inspection under the Public Records Act. A public body may not hold an
21 executive session except to consider one or more of the following:

1 * * *
2 (10) security, cybersecurity, or emergency response measures, the
3 disclosure of which could jeopardize public safety; or
4 (11) confidential business information relating to the interest rates for
5 publicly financed loans, provided that the public body is a State public body
6 and the creditor for the loan.
7 * * *
8 Sec. 4. LEGISLATIVE INTENT
9 It is the intent of the General Assembly that section 5 of this act amend 13
10 V.S.A. § 1026 to conform subdivision (a)(4) of that section with the
11 constitutional requirements articulated in the Supreme Court of Vermont
12 decision State v. Colby, 185 Vt. 464 (2009).
13 Sec. 5. 13 V.S.A. § 1026 is amended to read:
14 § 1026. DISORDERLY CONDUCT
15 (a) A person is guilty of disorderly conduct if he or she the person, with
16 intent to cause public inconvenience or annoyance, or recklessly creates a risk
17 thereof:
18 * * *
19 (4) without lawful authority, disturbs any lawful assembly or meeting of
20 persons; or
21 * * *
VT LEG #383813 v.1
HOUSE PROPOSAL OF AMENDMENT S.59
2025 Page 8 of 8
1 (c) As used in this section:
2 (1) “Disturbs any lawful assembly or meeting of persons” means
3 conduct that substantially impairs the effective conduct of an assembly or
4 meeting, including conduct that:
5 (A) causes an assembly or meeting to terminate prematurely; or
6 (B) consists of numerous and sustained efforts to disrupt an assembly
7 or meeting after being asked to desist.
8 (2) “Meeting” includes a meeting of a public body, as those terms are
9 defined in 1 V.S.A. § 310.
10 Sec. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE
11 This act shall take effect on passage.
