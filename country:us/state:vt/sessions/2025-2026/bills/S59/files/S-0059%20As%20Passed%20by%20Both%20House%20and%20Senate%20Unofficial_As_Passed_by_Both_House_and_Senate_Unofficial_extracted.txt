1 Sec. 3. 1 V.S.A. § 313 is amended to read:
2 § 313. EXECUTIVE SESSIONS
3 (a) No public body may hold or conclude an executive session from which
4 the public is excluded, except by the affirmative vote of two-thirds of its
5 members present in the case of any public body of State government or of a
6 majority of its members present in the case of any public body of a
7 municipality or other political subdivision. A motion to go into executive
8 session shall indicate the nature of the business of the executive session, and
9 no other matter may be considered in the executive session. Such The vote to
10 enter executive session shall be taken in the course of an open meeting and the
11 result of the vote recorded in the minutes. No formal or binding action shall be
12 taken in executive session except for actions relating to the securing of real
13 estate options under subdivision (2) of this subsection. Minutes of an
14 executive session need not be taken, but if they are, the minutes shall,
15 notwithstanding subsection 312(b) of this title, be exempt from public copying
16 and inspection under the Public Records Act. A public body may not hold an
17 executive session except to consider one or more of the following:
18 * * *
19 (10) security, cybersecurity, or emergency response measures, the
20 disclosure of which could jeopardize public safety; or

1 (11) confidential business information relating to the interest rates for
2 publicly financed loans, provided that the public body is a State public body
3 and the creditor for the loan.
4 * * *
5 Sec. 4. LEGISLATIVE INTENT
6 It is the intent of the General Assembly that section 5 of this act amend 13
7 V.S.A. § 1026 to conform subdivision (a)(4) of that section with the
8 constitutional requirements articulated in the Supreme Court of Vermont
9 decision State v. Colby, 185 Vt. 464 (2009).
10 Sec. 5. 13 V.S.A. § 1026 is amended to read:
11 § 1026. DISORDERLY CONDUCT
12 (a) A person is guilty of disorderly conduct if he or she the person, with
13 intent to cause public inconvenience or annoyance, or recklessly creates a risk
14 thereof:
15 * * *
16 (4) without lawful authority, disturbs any lawful assembly or meeting of
17 persons; or
18 * * *

1 (c) As used in this section:
2 (1) “Disturbs any lawful assembly or meeting of persons” means
3 conduct that substantially impairs the effective conduct of an assembly or
4 meeting, including conduct that:
5 (A) causes an assembly or meeting to terminate prematurely; or
6 (B) consists of numerous and sustained efforts to disrupt an assembly
7 or meeting after being asked to desist.
8 (2) “Meeting” includes a meeting of a public body, as those terms are
9 defined in 1 V.S.A. § 310.
10 Sec. 6. EFFECTIVE DATE
11 This act shall take effect on passage.
