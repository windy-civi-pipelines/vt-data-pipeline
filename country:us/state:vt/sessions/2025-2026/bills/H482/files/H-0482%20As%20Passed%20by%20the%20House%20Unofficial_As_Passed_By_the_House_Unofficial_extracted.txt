5 Sec. 1. 18 V.S.A. § 9384 is added to read:
6 § 9384. REDUCTION OR REALLOCATION OF REIMBURSEMENT
7 RATES; RISKS TO HEALTH INSURER SOLVENCY
8 (a) As used in this section:
9 (1) “Hospital” has the same meaning as in section 9451 of this title.
10 (2) “Hospital network” means a system comprising two or more
11 affiliated hospitals, and may include other health care professionals and
12 facilities, that derives 50 percent or more of its operating revenue, at the
13 consolidated network level, from Vermont hospitals and in which the affiliated
14 hospitals deliver health care services in a coordinated manner using an
15 integrated financial and governance structure.
16 (b) If the Green Mountain Care Board determines, after consultation with
17 the Commissioner of Financial Regulation, that a domestic health insurer faces
18 an acute and immediate threat to its solvency because its risk-based capital
19 level has triggered a regulatory action level event pursuant to 8 V.S.A. § 8304,
20 the Board may order a reduction of the insurer’s reimbursement rates to one or
21 more Vermont hospitals as set forth in subsection (c) of this section until such

1 time as the amount of the insurer’s risk-based capital exceeds the company
2 action level risk-based capital threshold defined in 8 V.S.A. § 8301.
3 (c)(1) The Board shall only order a reduction in the reimbursement rates to
4 a hospital that meets one or both of the following criteria:
5 (A) the hospital has more than 135 days’ cash on hand and had a
6 positive operating margin in the previous fiscal year; or
7 (B) the hospital is a member of a hospital network that, at the
8 consolidated network level, has more than 135 days’ cash on hand or had a
9 positive operating margin in the previous fiscal year, or both.
10 (2) The Board shall order a reduction in reimbursement rates to a
11 hospital pursuant to this subsection only to the extent necessary to remediate
12 the threat to the domestic health insurer’s solvency.
13 (3) In no event shall a reduction ordered by the Board pursuant to this
14 section result in a decrease to a hospital’s or hospital network’s projected days’
15 cash on hand to below 125 days.

2 hospital, or other person, directly or indirectly, owns, controls, holds with the
3 power to vote, or holds proxies representing 20 percent or more of the voting
4 securities or membership interest or other governing interest of the hospital or
5 other controlled entity.
6 (2)(A) The Board may, upon finding that a hospital has made a material
7 misrepresentation in information or documents provided to the Board or that a
8 hospital is materially noncompliant with the budget established by the Board
9 pursuant to this section, appoint an independent observer with respect to any
10 matter related to the Board’s review or enforcement under this section if the
11 Board believes that doing so is in the public interest. At the direction of the
12 Board, the independent observer may monitor the hospital’s operations, obtain
13 information from the hospital, and report findings and recommendations to the
14 Board.

16 (2) shall have the right to receive copies of all materials related to the Board’s
17 review under this section and the hospital shall provide any information
18 requested by the independent observer, including any information regarding
19 the hospital’s participation in a hospital network. The independent observer
20 shall share information provided by the hospital with the Board and with the

7 Sec. 3. INDEPENDENT HOSPITAL OBSERVER AUTHORITY;
