1 S.32
2 Introduced by Senators Hardy, Beck, Heffernan, Watson and Williams
3 Referred to Committee on
4 Date:
5 Subject: Fish and wildlife; fishing; free license; persons with developmental
6 disabilities
7 Statement of purpose of bill as introduced: This bill proposes to provide a free
8 permanent fishing license to Vermont residents who are persons with
9 developmental disabilities.
10 An act relating to providing a free fishing license to persons with
11 developmental disabilities
12 It is hereby enacted by the General Assembly of the State of Vermont:
13 Sec. 1. 10 V.S.A. § 4255(c) is amended to read:
14 (c) A permanent or free license may be secured on application to the
15 Department by a person qualifying as follows:
16 * * *
17 (2) A person who is legally blind who is a Vermont resident may receive
18 a free permanent fishing license upon submittal of proper proof of blindness as
19 the Commissioner shall require. A person who is legally blind who is a

1 resident in a state that provides a reciprocal privilege for Vermont residents
2 may receive a free one-year fishing license.
3 (3) A Vermont resident with paraplegia as defined in subdivision
4 4001(30) of this title or a permanent, severe, physical mobility disability
5 certified by a physician may receive a free permanent fishing license or, if the
6 person qualifies for a hunting license, a free combination hunting and fishing
7 license. A person with paraplegia or a person certified by a physician to have
8 permanent, severe, physical mobility disability who is a resident of a state that
9 provides a reciprocal privilege for Vermont residents may receive a free one-
10 year fishing license or, if the person qualifies for a hunting license, a free one-
11 year combination fishing and hunting license.
12 (4) A Vermont resident who is a veteran of the U.S. Armed Forces and
13 who is, or ever has been, 60 percent disabled as a result of a service-connected
14 disability may receive a free fishing, hunting, or combination hunting and
15 fishing license that shall include all big game licenses, except for a moose
16 license, upon presentation of a certificate issued by the veterans’
17 administration so certifying. A resident of a state that provides a reciprocal
18 privilege for Vermont veterans and who would qualify for a free license under
19 this subdivision if the person were a Vermont resident may receive a free one-
20 year fishing, hunting, or combination hunting and fishing license.

1 (5) A person participating in a fishing tournament for Special Olympics
2 may receive a free fishing license valid for that event.
3 * * *
4 (7) A certified citizen of a Native American Indian tribe that has been
5 recognized by the State pursuant to 1 V.S.A. chapter 23 may receive free of
6 charge one or all of the permanent fishing, hunting, or trapping licenses set
7 forth in subdivisions (1)(A)–(D) of this subsection if qualified for the license
8 and upon submission of a current and valid tribal identification card.
9 (8) A person with developmental disabilities who is a Vermont resident
10 may receive a free permanent fishing license upon submission to the
11 Commissioner of a statement signed by the person’s physician certifying that
12 the person meets the definition of a person with development disabilities. “A
13 person with developmental disabilities” has the same meaning as in 18 V.S.A.
14 § 9302.
15 Sec. 2. EFFECTIVE DATE
16 This act shall take effect on July 1, 2025.
